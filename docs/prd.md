# R182 Commander - Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Executive Summary

### Product Name

R182 Commander

### Mission

Transform a newly endorsed Cessna R182RG pilot into a confident aircraft commander through scenario-based learning built upon the aircraft Pilot Operating Handbook (POH), systems knowledge, emergency procedures, and operational decision-making.

### Primary Goal

Teach aircraft command rather than POH memorisation.

### Primary Audience

- Newly endorsed R182RG pilots
- Aircraft owners
- Pilots transitioning into retractable gear aircraft

### Secondary Audience

- Flight instructors
- Aviation clubs
- Aircraft syndicates

---

# Product Vision

Most aviation training products focus on memorising facts.

R182 Commander focuses on:

- Understanding the aircraft
- Understanding systems
- Threat recognition
- Decision making
- Emergency management
- Aircraft command

The application should feel like an aircraft commander development program rather than a digital POH.

---

# Learning Philosophy

The learning model is:

Knowledge → Understanding → Application → Command

Every lesson, assessment, and scenario must support progression through these stages.

The objective is not to answer questions correctly.

The objective is to make safe and effective operational decisions.

---

# Core Principles

## Principle 1

Understand the aircraft.

## Principle 2

Understand how systems interact.

## Principle 3

Recognise threats early.

## Principle 4

Make sound operational decisions.

## Principle 5

Use the POH correctly.

# Assessment Philosophy

The application will prioritise operational competence over rote recall.

Scenarios and assessments will evaluate:

- Recognition
- Diagnosis
- Prioritisation
- Decision Making
- POH Compliance

The emphasis is on judgement and aircraft command.

---

# Success Criteria

After approximately ten hours of structured use, a pilot should be able to:

- Explain major aircraft systems
- Operate the aircraft safely
- Apply POH limitations correctly
- Manage common emergencies
- Recognise operational threats
- Demonstrate sound aircraft command judgement
